The 3 phases

Phase 1 — High Deficit Weeks 1–6
600 kcal deficit · 2,400 kcal · 0.75–1 lb/week
Eat ~2,400 kcal/day. Protein 185g minimum. Carbs ~175g, fat ~65g. Weigh daily, track 7-day average.
Phase 2 — Moderate Deficit Weeks 7–13
400 kcal deficit · 2,600 kcal · 0.5–0.75 lb/week
Reduce deficit to 400 kcal. Consider a 3–5 day diet break around weeks 9–10. Carbs increase to ~210g to maintain climbing performance.
Phase 3 — Dial In Weeks 14–18
150–200 kcal deficit · ~2,800–2,850 kcal
Near maintenance. Abs reveal as the final fat layer thins. Prioritise sleep (7–9 hrs). If ahead of schedule, shift to maintenance earlier.
Timeline: You need to lose ~18 lbs to hit 11% BF. The revised Phase 2 deficit projects ~13–14 lbs across phases 1–2, with Phase 3 closing the rest. Abs typically show at 12–13% — you may hit the goal before August.
On eating less than planned: If you feel full and are climbing well, your body is self-regulating. Use the weekly average as your guide — 0.5–1.5 lbs/week drop is ideal. More than 2 lbs/week consistently, eat more. Flat for 2 weeks, drop 150–200 kcal.

The rules that matter

Track weekly averages
Weigh every morning, average the 7 readings. Ignore daily swings of 2–4 lbs — they are mostly water.
Stall protocol
If weekly average doesn't drop for 2+ weeks, reduce calories by 150 kcal and reassess after 2 more weeks.
Protein first
185g/day every day, regardless of phase. Biggest single lever for keeping muscle on a cut.
Sleep is mandatory
7–9 hours. Poor sleep raises cortisol, tanks testosterone, increases hunger, stalls fat loss.
Scale vs performance
If weight drops but climbing feels strong, you're fine. If strength fades at the wall, eat more.
Patience in Phase 3
Abs appear when the final fat layer thins — don't panic and slash calories in week 16.

Sunday Prep

Do this every Sunday to set up the whole week. Steps are in the order you should do them — some things can sit in the fridge while you do others.

Big-batch reminder: Sunday prep does NOT include cooking the main dinners. Those are cooked fresh on Monday (1 portion) and Tuesday + Thursday (3 portions each). Sunday is just marinade, boiled eggs, snack portions, and any grains that keep well.
Week A timing: Everything here takes about 35 min. Rice and eggs can run simultaneously. Marinating takes 2 min of hands-on time then just sits in the fridge.
1
Cook a small pot of rice (Mon portion only)
Rinse 55g dry jasmine rice, cook with 85ml water. Spread on a tray to cool — cold rice reheats better. Store in one container. Tuesday and Thursday batch rice is cooked fresh on those nights.
2
Marinate all chicken thighs
Whisk: 4 tbsp soy sauce, 3 tbsp mirin, 1.5 tbsp honey, 1 tsp sesame oil, 3 minced garlic cloves. Pour over all 1kg chicken in a zip bag. Seal, shake to coat, refrigerate. Monday: cook 250g. Tuesday: cook remaining 750g as the big batch (3 portions).
3
Slice and store beef
Slice 600g beef thinly against the grain — easier when the beef is cold and firm. Store in a container with a light coating of soy sauce and sesame oil. Cook all Thursday as the big batch (3 portions).
4
Hard-boil 6 eggs
Rolling boil, lower eggs gently, cook exactly 6.5 minutes. Transfer immediately to ice bath for 5 min. Store UNPEELED in the fridge — they keep 5 days and peel more cleanly cold. These are Snack 1 Mon–Fri (2 eggs per day).
5
Blanch broccoli
Cut into florets. Boil 2 min, drain immediately, run under cold water to stop cooking. Store in a container. Reheat by tossing in a hot pan for 1 min each night — much quicker than cooking from raw.
6
Portion cottage cheese snacks
Divide 2 tubs of cottage cheese into 5 containers of ~150g each. These are Snack 2 Mon–Fri. Do not add blueberries yet — add them fresh when eating or they go soft.
7
Tuesday + Thursday big-batch reminder
Tuesday evening: cook 750g chicken + 165g dry rice + 450g broccoli (3× sauce). Portion into 3 containers: Tue dinner / Wed dinner / Wed lunch. Thursday evening: cook 540g beef + 240g dry noodles + 450g broccoli (3× sauce). Portion into 3 containers: Thu dinner / Fri dinner / Fri lunch.
Week B timing: About 35 min. Shape kofta first so they can chill while you do everything else. Bulgur cooks itself once you pour the water.
1
Mix and shape all kofta patties
Combine 600g lamb mince with 1 tsp each cumin, coriander, smoked paprika, cinnamon + 2 minced garlic cloves + salt, pepper + small handful chopped parsley. Mix thoroughly with your hands for 2 min — the more you work it, the better they hold together on the pan. Shape into 10–12 oval patties (~55g each). Cover, refrigerate. Cook ALL of them Thursday.
2
Marinate all chicken thighs
Mix all shawarma spices (1 tsp each cumin, smoked paprika, turmeric, coriander; ½ tsp cinnamon, garlic powder, salt) with a glug of olive oil and juice of 1 lemon. Coat all 1kg chicken thoroughly. Zip bag, refrigerate. Monday: cook 250g. Tuesday: cook remaining 750g as the big batch (3 portions).
3
Make yogurt sauce
300g Greek yogurt + 1 small garlic clove minced + juice of half a lemon + salt + pinch of cumin. Stir and store in a jar. Keeps 5 days. Used as sauce for both the chicken and the kofta, and as a snack dip if needed.
4
Cook bulgur for the whole week
Bring 340ml water to a boil. Add 225g bulgur wheat + 1 tsp salt. Cover, remove from heat, rest 15 min. Fluff with a fork, cool, portion into 3 containers. These cover Thursday dinner, Friday dinner, and Friday lunch — already done.
5
Hard-boil 6 eggs
Rolling boil, 6.5 min exactly, ice bath 5 min. Store unpeeled in the fridge. Snack 1 Mon–Fri — peel just before eating.
6
Portion cottage cheese snacks
Divide 2 tubs into 5 × ~150g containers. Add blueberries fresh when eating — do not pre-mix.
7
Tuesday + Thursday big-batch reminder
Tuesday evening: cook 750g chicken + 210g dry couscous (3× yogurt sauce already made). Portion into 3 containers: Tue dinner / Wed dinner / Wed lunch. Thursday evening: cook ALL kofta patties + reheat pre-cooked bulgur. Portion into 3 containers: Thu dinner / Fri dinner / Fri lunch. Kofta improves overnight — Fri dinner tastes better than Thu.
Week C timing: ~40 min hands-on. Rub the pork first so it can go in the fridge. The slow cooker doesn't start Sunday — you start it Wednesday morning before work. It cooks while you climb.
1
Rub the pork shoulder
Mix: 2 tsp smoked paprika + 1 tsp each cumin, garlic powder, chilli powder + 1 tsp salt. Coat pork shoulder all over with the dry rub. Place in the slow cooker insert, cover, refrigerate overnight. Wednesday morning before work: add 100ml water + 3 tbsp apple cider vinegar, set to LOW, leave for 8 hours. Done when you get home from climbing.
2
Make chipotle marinade and coat chicken
Blend: 2 chipotle peppers in adobo + 1 tbsp adobo sauce + juice of 2 limes + 3 garlic cloves + 1 tsp cumin + salt. Pour over all 880g chicken in a zip bag. Seal, refrigerate overnight minimum. Monday: cook 220g. Tuesday: cook remaining 660g as the big batch (3 portions).
3
Cook rice (Monday portion only)
Cook 100g dry rice. Cool and store in one container for Monday. Tuesday and Thursday batch rice is cooked fresh on those nights alongside the main protein.
4
Season and portion black beans
Drain 2 cans black beans. Warm in a pan with 1 tsp cumin, ½ tsp garlic powder, salt, 2 tbsp water. Mash slightly so some are broken — better texture. Cool, portion into 4 containers. Used Mon–Thu across lunches and dinners.
5
Shred cabbage for slaw
Finely shred half a head of cabbage. Store DRY in a container. Do NOT dress ahead — dress each individual portion fresh with lime juice + salt + small spoon of mayo when serving. Dressed cabbage goes soggy within hours; dry cabbage lasts 4 days.
6
Hard-boil 6 eggs
Rolling boil, 6.5 min exactly, ice bath 5 min. Store unpeeled. Snack 1 Mon–Fri.
7
Portion cottage cheese snacks
Divide 2 tubs into 5 × ~150g containers. Add blueberries fresh when eating.
8
Wednesday morning + Tuesday big-batch reminder
Wednesday morning before leaving: pork in slow cooker, LOW, 8 hrs. Pulled pork portions Thu dinner / Fri dinner / Fri lunch — done automatically. Tuesday evening: grill 660g chipotle chicken + 150g dry rice + 1.5 cans beans. Portion into 3 containers: Tue dinner / Wed dinner / Wed lunch.
